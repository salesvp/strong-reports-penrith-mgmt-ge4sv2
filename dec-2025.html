EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

STRONG Pilates Penrith ended Q4 2025 with 130 total members, of which 124 were active and 6 suspended. The studio maintained a stable membership base throughout its first full quarter of operations, with active membership rates above 95% through October, November, and December.

Revenue

The studio generated $96,041 in gross revenue during Q4 2025, with monthly revenue ranging from $29,887 in December to $33,391 in November. Membership revenue accounted for 87.7% of total quarterly revenue at $84,211, establishing a strong recurring revenue foundation. Package sales contributed $6,171 (6.4%) and intro offers generated $4,776 (5.0%) across the quarter.

Utilisation

Session utilisation averaged 32.89% across Q4, declining from 36.74% in October to 29.94% in December. This reflected both seasonal attendance patterns and the strategic reduction of some classes to optimise operational expenses. The studio operated well below the optimal 70-75% utilisation range, providing substantial capacity for membership growth without requiring additional class inventory.

Sales vs Cancellations

The studio sold 70 memberships during Q4 while cancelling 38, with monthly sales declining from 38 in October to 14 in December. Member attrition improved from 10.19% in October to 8.46% in December, trending toward the sub-7% target range but requiring further improvement to reach exceptional retention levels.

Key Issues

  • Sales momentum declining: Membership sales dropped 63% from October (38 units) to December (14 units), reflecting post-opening normalisation but also highlighting conversion challenges that require process refinement and execution consistency.
  • Lead generation pipeline weakening: First visits declined 51% from 95 in October to 47 in December, indicating the need to rebuild top-of-funnel activity through promotional initiatives and social media strategy.
  • Attrition above target: December attrition of 8.46% remains above the sub-7% target, requiring sustained focus on retention processes and member engagement to drive rates toward the exceptional 5% benchmark.
  • Utilisation below optimal range: Q4 average of 32.89% sits well below the 70-75% optimal utilisation target, though this provides capacity runway for growth, the gap indicates significant membership expansion opportunity.
  • Conversion execution gaps: Declining sales trajectory despite solid team and systems in place points to inconsistencies in sales execution, trainer-FOH alignment, and customer journey follow-through.
  • Seasonal headwinds: December revenue decline to $29,887 and January suspension spike to 12 members reflect expected seasonal patterns that will require proactive engagement to navigate Q1 2026.

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • Starter Pass promotion: 15 days for $50 offer currently running through mid-February, designed to drive trial activity and rebuild first visit momentum following Q4 decline.
  • STRONG Challenge initiative: Free challenge programme with designated staff leadership to create buzz, engage new prospects and existing members, and generate word-of-mouth referrals.
  • Acquisition and retention processes rolled out: Systematic frameworks deployed across pipeline management, member engagement, and conversion optimisation to drive consistency in customer journey execution.
  • Front of house restructure: Team reorganised with daily actionable tasks assigned specifically to drive membership growth momentum and improve conversion rates.
  • Process and sales training programme: Ongoing training initiatives for trainers and FOH staff to build consistency, improve sales execution, and increase conversion rates to satisfactory levels.
  • Social media strategy formulation: Developing content and engagement approach to support lead generation, rebuild first visit pipeline, and amplify growth initiatives like STRONG Challenge.
  • Systemisation with human focus: Automating operational elements while maintaining deliberate human interaction, guidance, and follow-through at critical customer journey touchpoints to increase conversions.

Expected Outcomes

  • Rebuild first visit pipeline from Q4 decline (95→47) through Starter Pass promotion and STRONG Challenge, targeting return to consistent double-digit weekly trial activity.
  • Reduce attrition from 8.46% toward sub-7% target through deployed retention processes, improved member engagement, and systematic follow-through on attendance patterns and milestone recognition.
  • Improve membership sales conversion rates through sustained sales training, trainer-FOH consistency, and refined execution of acquisition processes until performance reaches satisfactory levels.
  • Optimise utilisation from 32.89% toward 70-75% target range as membership growth initiatives generate new member additions and fill available class capacity.
  • Execute consistent daily operational tasks and growth initiatives that support sustainable membership expansion beyond post-opening volatility into Q1 2026 and beyond.

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Penrith completed its first full quarter of operations with 130 total members, $96,041 in revenue, and a membership-driven business model generating 87.7% of revenue from recurring dues. While the studio established operational foundations during Q4 2025, the declining sales trajectory from 38 memberships in October to 14 in December, combined with first visits dropping from 95 to 47, indicates the site is underperforming relative to its potential. The team is solid, and the foundational systems are now in place—the challenge is execution consistency and conversion optimisation.

In response, comprehensive interventions have been deployed: acquisition and retention processes rolled out, front of house restructured with daily actionable tasks assigned to drive membership growth momentum, and key staff designated to lead growth initiatives including the STRONG Challenge and ongoing Starter Pass promotion. The approach balances systemisation and automation with deliberate human interaction throughout the customer journey to increase conversions. This strategy requires ongoing consistency between trainers and FOH, continuous training on processes, and sustained sales training until conversion rates reach satisfactory levels. The social media strategy is being formulated to support lead generation, while the studio's 32.89% utilisation provides ample capacity to absorb the growth these interventions are designed to generate.

Key Takeaways

  • Site underperforming with declining sales (38→14 memberships) and first visits (95→47), though solid team and systems now in place
  • Comprehensive interventions deployed: acquisition/retention processes, restructured FOH, daily actionable tasks, and designated initiative leaders
  • Strategy emphasises human interaction and conversion optimisation alongside systemisation and automation
  • Path forward requires execution consistency, ongoing process training, and sustained sales development until conversions improve

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

STRONG Pilates Penrith ended the quarter with 126 total members (116 active, 10 suspended). The membership base declined modestly from 131 in November to 126 in January, representing a 3.8% contraction. Active rates fluctuated between 82.3% and 94.7%, with December's seasonal dip to 82.3% recovering to 92.1% by January as members returned from holiday suspensions.

Revenue

The studio generated $98,059 in gross revenue across November-January, with monthly revenue ranging from $29,887 in December to $34,781 in January. Membership revenue accounted for 85.1% of total quarterly revenue at $83,466, establishing a strong recurring revenue foundation. Package sales contributed $7,881 (8.0%) and intro offers generated $4,629 (4.7%), with intro offers surging in January following the holiday period.

Utilisation

Booking utilisation averaged 38.6% across the quarter, declining from 41.39% in November to 37.46% in January. Attendance utilisation tracked consistently lower at 32.7% quarterly average, reaching a low of 29.94% in December before recovering to 33.76% in January. The studio operated well below the optimal 70-85% booking and 70-75% attendance targets, providing substantial capacity for membership growth.

Sales vs Cancellations

The studio sold 48 memberships during the quarter while cancelling 42, with monthly sales ranging from 14 (December) to 18 (November). Member attrition fluctuated between 8.46% and 12.31%, improving to 8.46% in December before increasing to 11.63% in January, remaining above the sub-7% target throughout the period. First visits demonstrated significant volatility.

Key Issues

  • Attrition above target: Current rates of 8.46-12.31% exceed sub-7% target, requiring focused retention protocols to reduce member churn.
  • Lead generation volatility: First visits dropped 52% from November (98) to December (47), creating pipeline instability constraining membership growth.
  • Booking utilisation below target: Operating at 37-41% capacity versus 70-85% optimal range indicates substantial unused revenue opportunity.
  • Attendance utilisation gap: Averaging under 34% against 70-75% healthy threshold highlights member engagement and stickiness challenges requiring intervention.
  • Minimal membership growth: Monthly sales of 14-18 offset by 11-16 cancellations produce insufficient net growth to expand base.
  • Seasonal revenue vulnerability: December revenue dropped to $29,887, demonstrating exposure to holiday-period disruptions in member activity and lead generation.

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • Recovr retention software: Driving systematic retention through automated engagement, suspension management, and pipeline tracking to reduce attrition toward sub-7% target.
  • STRONG Starter Pass promotion: Generating positive trial response with conversions expected mid-to-late February, rebuilding lead generation momentum from January's 51 intro offers.
  • STRONG Intro Offer transition: Mid-February launch of 5 days for $50 offer increases conversion rates, directly addressing first visit volatility and pipeline challenges.
  • STRONG Challenge launching: Drives class attendance, member engagement, and retention by creating community momentum and consistent booking behaviour to improve utilisation.
  • FOH restructure and sales training: New customer journey structure with intensive training optimises pipeline efficiency and conversion from first visits to memberships.
  • Social media calendar system: Ensures consistent, on-brand posting in quantity and quality, enhancing digital presence to drive local awareness and first visits.
  • Member survey and community engagement: Gathers data on class times, creche needs, and run/walk club interest to optimise scheduling and build member stickiness.
  • Westfield guerilla marketing: Executes targeted local marketing initiatives to drive foot traffic, awareness, and first visits, directly addressing pipeline volatility challenges.

Expected Outcomes

  • Reduce attrition from 8.46-12.31% range toward sub-7% target through Recovr protocols and STRONG Challenge engagement initiatives.
  • Rebuild first visits to consistent 80-100+ monthly via Starter Pass, new intro offer, social media, and Westfield marketing.
  • Optimise booking utilisation from 37-41% toward 70-85% target through membership growth and community engagement driving consistent class attendance.
  • Improve attendance utilisation from under 34% toward 70-75% healthy threshold via retention focus and community activities reducing no-shows.
  • Convert January momentum (51 intro offers, 82 first visits) into sustained membership sales of 20-25+ units monthly through systematic execution.

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Penrith navigated a challenging quarter with membership levels declining from 131 to 126 members (-3.8%) despite maintaining stable monthly sales of 14-18 memberships. Revenue totalled $98,059 across November-January, with membership revenue consistently contributing 81-88% of monthly totals and providing a stable financial baseline. The studio demonstrated recovery capacity, with January's gross revenue rebounding to $34,781 from December's seasonal low of $29,887, supported by a 200% increase in intro offer sales and improved revenue per visit ($26.03, up 15.5% from November). However, significant operational challenges persist: booking utilisation averaged only 38.6% (well below the 70-85% target), attendance utilisation remained under 34% (far from the 70-75% healthy threshold), and attrition rates fluctuated between 8.46-12.31% (above the sub-7% target).

The quarter revealed critical vulnerabilities in lead generation and retention systems. First visits collapsed 52% from November (98) to December (47) before partially recovering to 82 in January, creating pipeline volatility that directly limits membership sales potential. The studio's capacity for growth is evident in its substantial unused booking capacity (operating at less than 40% utilisation), but realising this potential requires systematic improvements across acquisition, conversion, and retention processes. Immediate priorities include stabilising first visit generation to rebuild the sales pipeline, implementing retention protocols to reduce attrition toward the sub-7% target, and optimising class scheduling and member engagement to increase booking utilisation toward the 70-85% range. With foundational systems like Recovr retention software now in place and initiatives including the STRONG Challenge launching to drive engagement, the studio has the tools necessary to address these challenges and convert excess capacity into sustainable membership growth throughout 2026.

Key Takeaways

  • Membership base contracted 3.8% to 126 members with quarterly revenue of $98,059, highlighting the need for stronger acquisition and retention
  • Booking utilisation of 38.6% and attendance utilisation under 34% indicate significant growth capacity requiring systematic member engagement improvements
  • Attrition rates above 8% throughout most of the quarter demand focused retention protocols to achieve the sub-7% target consistently
  • January's recovery signals (51 intro offers sold, 82 first visits, $26.03 revenue per visit) provide momentum to build upon with consistent execution of sales and retention systems in 2026

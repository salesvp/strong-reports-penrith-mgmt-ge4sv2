Executive Summary
Current State

Membership: The membership base stabilised across Q1 at 125–126 members after two consecutive quarters of contraction, with active rate recovering to 91.2% in March. However, 44 sales against 43 cancellations produced near-zero net growth — the base is being maintained, not expanded.

Revenue: The quarter delivered $92,837 in gross revenue, marginally above the prior period's $92,413 and halting the quarter-on-quarter decline. January's $34,781 remained the strongest month since opening, with March recovering to $30,311 driven by record package revenue of $3,400. Membership revenue contributed 80.9–86.7% monthly.

Utilisation: Booking utilisation averaged 36.37% across Q1, declining from 37.46% in January to 34.03% in March — well below the 75–85% optimal range. Attendance utilisation followed the same trajectory, easing to 30.51% in March. The booking-to-attendance gap remained tight at 3.52–3.78%, indicating strong attendance stickiness among members who book.

Sales vs Cancellations: Attrition improved to 9.76% in March from the prior period's peak of 12.90% in February — the first positive reversal after three months of deterioration. Conversion from intro offers to memberships remains relatively strong; the core constraint is top-of-funnel volume, with first visits stalled at 47 monthly in February and March.

Key Issues
  • Attrition still above target: March's 9.76% improved from February's 12.90% but remains above the sub-7% benchmark.
  • Top-of-funnel volume stalled: First visits flat at 47 monthly in February and March, constraining the entire acquisition pipeline.
  • Net membership growth near zero: 44 sales against 43 cancellations across Q1 means the base is maintained, not grown.
  • Utilisation well below target: Booking at 34–37% and attendance at 30–34% sit far below the 75–85% and 70–75% benchmarks.
  • Paid marketing budget constraint: Low member count and high location expenses limit ability to fund the sustained paid push required for growth.
Active Interventions Being Deployed
  • STRONG 25 Tracker: Targeting the 65–75% of cancellations occurring within a member's first 25 classes by gamifying early engagement and building long-term habit formation.
  • Acquisition Playbook launched: Phase 1 of the Operations Manual provides FOH with standardised sales scripts, SMS templates, and pipeline management tools to streamline conversion.
  • Local Area Marketing activations: Panthers meet-and-greet distributed 100 intro offers, Westfield partnership expanding across EDMs, socials, membership apps, and Visual Merchandising Box.
  • New promotional pricing: 7-for-$50 intro offer and buy-3-get-5 class pack at $99 launching end of April to stimulate trial activity and top-of-funnel volume.
  • 1000 Rep Challenge on 18th April: Open to first-timers, designed to generate buzz and capture new prospects for conversion into memberships.
  • HQ performance team visit: Upcoming on-site support to further optimise sales execution, pipeline management, and social media processes across the studio.
Expected Outcomes
  • Reduce attrition toward sub-7% target through STRONG 25 Tracker engagement and continued FOH retention focus.
  • Rebuild first visit volume through LAM activations, Westfield partnerships, and promotional pricing to expand the acquisition pipeline.
  • Secure sustained paid marketing investment as the critical lever to drive real membership growth ahead of the winter dip.
  • Convert 1000 Rep Challenge and Panthers event prospects into memberships, leveraging the proven intro-to-membership conversion pathway.

Summary & Path Forward

Q1 2026 delivered cautious progress: the membership base stabilised after two quarters of contraction, attrition reversed from its February peak, and quarterly revenue marginally exceeded the prior period. Sales, pipeline, and socials processes are effectively managed, and conversion from intro offers to memberships is sound. The core issue is unambiguous: top-of-funnel volume is insufficient, with first visits stalled at 47 monthly. An upcoming HQ performance team visit will provide further on-site support.

Organic activations — the 1000 Rep Challenge on 18th April, the Panthers meet-and-greet distributing 100 intro offers, Westfield partnerships including the LSKD store opening collaboration — build word of mouth and local buzz. However, sustained paid marketing is fundamentally required to drive real membership growth. Low member count and revenue, coupled with high location and space expenses, create the budget constraint that makes this difficult to solve. Securing marketing investment is the critical unlock heading into the winter dip.

Key Takeaways
  • Q1 delivered the first positive directional signals — membership stabilised, attrition reversed, and revenue marginally improved quarter-on-quarter
  • Top-of-funnel volume is the unambiguous core constraint; conversion quality is sound but first visits remain stalled at 47 monthly
  • Organic activations and LAM build local buzz but sustained paid marketing is fundamentally required to drive real membership growth
  • Securing marketing investment is the critical unlock — low member count and high location expenses create the budget constraint that must be resolved
